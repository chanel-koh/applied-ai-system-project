# Pet Care Reference

## Grooming
- A grooming appointment typically takes 1.5 hours.
- Poodles usually need a haircut every 4 weeks.
- Dogs with long coats may need grooming every 4 to 6 weeks.
- Cats with long fur may need grooming once per month.
- High activity dogs may benefit from more frequent grooming and coat trims.
- Senior pets may need grooming checks for mats and skin health more often.
- Poodles and double-coated breeds often require regular professional grooming.

## Feeding
- Most adult dogs eat twice a day.
- Most adult cats eat two to three times a day.
- Growing puppies often need three small meals per day.
- Older pets may benefit from smaller, more frequent meals for energy balance.

## Medication
- Daily medication is common for chronic conditions.
- Weekly medication is sometimes used for preventive treatments.
- Medications should be scheduled at consistent times to improve adherence.
- Medication reminders work best when tied to regular events like feeding or walks.

## Appointment guidance
- A vaccination or vet appointment often lasts about 30 minutes to 1 hour.
- A grooming appointment should be scheduled at least a week in advance for busy salons.
- Vet visits should be scheduled during clinic hours and may take longer for new patients.
- Pet owners should allow buffer time for travel, check-in, and prep time when planning appointments.

## Enrichment and behavior
- Daily play and mental enrichment are important for high activity breeds.
- Short training sessions can improve behavior and decrease anxiety.
- Enrichment tasks can be scheduled around regular feeding or walking routines.
